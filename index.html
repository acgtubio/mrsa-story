Simulating the spread of Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus

Adrian Christopher Tubio

Staphylococcus aureus is a type of bacteria commonly found on the human skin, in the nose, armpit, groin, and other areas. Most of the time, this type of bacteria does not cause any harm. However, in the right conditions, infections may occur. People colonized with this bacteria do not exhibit any symptoms and are still able to spread this bacteria through physical contact.
